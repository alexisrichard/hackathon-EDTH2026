EDTH 2026 — Baltic project, pre-event data state

All data sources collected or wired up. Bulk downloads queued for overnight. Repo: github.com/alexisrichard/hackathon-EDTH2026 · onboarding instructions in ONBOARDING.md.

Where the data lives

  • S3 bucket: s3://edth2026-baltic/ (eu-west-3, ~31 GB now, ~400 GB after overnight)
  • Small / committed in repo: data/geo/, data/reference/
  • Large stuff (AIS Parquet, satellite, KSE raw): S3 only — fetch with scripts/common/sync_from_s3.py

What we have ✓

AIS (vessel positions)

  • Danish (DMA, bulk Parquet) — 118 days covering every named incident window. Full 2022→present backfill runs overnight (paused at the user's request to free bandwidth).
  • AISStream (live WebSocket) — tested at 38 msg/sec, for Sunday's live demo.

Satellite imagery

  • Sentinel-1 SAR — 441 scenes catalogued, 9 incident-AOI crops downloaded
  • Sentinel-2 optical — same: 441 catalogued, 9 true-color crops

Criticality / infrastructure layers (36 files)

  • OSM: submarine cables, power cables, pipelines, ports, naval bases, refineries, offshore wind, platforms, TSS, restricted areas, anchorages, lighthouses, wrecks, fairways, buoys
  • EMODnet: pipelines, wind farms, cables (national merged)
  • HELCOM: 4,932 shipping accidents, 5,838 oil spills, dredging, disposal, fishing intensity
  • Natural Earth: countries, coastlines, ports, rivers, urban areas, ocean
  • Marine Regions: 12 Baltic-state EEZ polygons
  • GMRT bathymetry: 29 MB Baltic sea-floor depth grid
  • Chokepoints: 9 manual entries (Skagerrak, Øresund, Great Belt, Gulf of Finland, Kaliningrad, etc.)

Reference / lookup

  • Incidents: 9 well-sourced events Sep 2022 → Jan 2026 (Nord Stream, Balticconnector, Yi Peng 3 ×2, Eagle S, Vezhen, Cinia, Fitburg, Sventoji-Liepaja)
  • Sanctions: 1,773 maritime entries (OFAC SDN 1,480 vessels + UK OFSI 219 + EU FSF 74)
  • KSE shadow-fleet PDF: 72 ship managers + 48 buyers parsed
  • Marine weather + sea state: 9 incident windows × hourly samples
  • Equasis lookup: per-MMSI/IMO registry pull (vessel name, ownership, manager, ISM, classification, port history)
  • Global Fishing Watch: vessel registry + event types (port-visits, loitering, encounters, gaps, fishing) — runs overnight
  • 10 Kaggle datasets: drone-video SDS, HRSID, LS-SSDD, ships-in-satellite, MASATI, AFO, Kattegat AIS, port activity, Ukraine events, more — download overnight

Overnight job queued

powershell -File scripts\overnight.ps1
  • Danish AIS full backfill 2022→present (~70 h)
  • 10 Kaggle datasets (~30–60 min)
  • GFW per-vessel events (~10 min)

All idempotent — safe to interrupt and restart. Logs land at data/cache/overnight_*.log.

Honest gaps ~

  • KSE vessel-level list — public PDF is aggregate stats only. Emailed KSE Institute (Hilgenstock / Kravtsev) requesting the per-vessel IMO dataset; awaiting reply.
  • Finnish bulk historical AIS — Digitraffic API works per-MMSI but their archive depth is shallow. Danish AIS partially covers Gulf of Finland anyway.
  • Orange Marine cable routes — Alexis's CEO contact; potential authoritative data if pursued.
