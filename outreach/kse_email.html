SUBJECT

Research data request for Anatolii Kravtsev / Benjamin Hilgenstock — Russian Shadow Fleet Tracker vessel list (EDTH 2026 hackathon)

BODY

Dear KSE team,

Could this please be forwarded to Anatolii Kravtsev (Research Lead on Shadow Fleet) or Benjamin Hilgenstock (Director, Center for Geoeconomics and Resilience)? My request relates directly to their published Russian Shadow Fleet Tracker.

I'd like to ask whether KSE Institute would share the vessel-level dataset behind the tracker — the list of tankers your team identifies as "shadow fleet" under the G7+-link and IG P&I criteria, with their IMO numbers and (where available) ship manager, registered owner, and flag.

Context

I'm a French engineer and entrepreneur — background in telecoms, cyber, and submarine cables. With two colleagues, I'm competing at the European Defence Tech Hackathon (EDTH) in Paris on June 12-14, 2026.

Our project is a maritime cueing engine for Baltic undersea-infrastructure protection. We ingest AIS, criticality layers (cables, pipelines, naval bases), and sanctions data to prioritise where ISR sensors should be tasked — directly motivated by the post-2022 Baltic cable-cut incidents: Nord Stream, Balticconnector, C-Lion1, Estlink 2 / Eagle S, Latvia–Sweden / Vezhen, and the recent Elisa / Fitburg case.

Why your data

OFAC SDN and UK OFSI cover Iranian, Cuban, and Venezuelan tankers well — but they have a clear blind spot for the Russian shadow-fleet vessels actually implicated in those incidents: Eagle S, Yi Peng 3, Newnew Polar Bear, Fitburg, Vezhen. None of them are on OFAC. Your dataset would dramatically improve our ability to surface the right vessels during the demo.

Scope of use

  • Strictly the hackathon prototype and the demo to judges on June 14
  • Non-commercial, no public redistribution
  • Full attribution to KSE Institute in any pitch deck, demo, or follow-up materials
  • Happy to delete the data after the event, or follow whatever conditions you specify
  • Any technical format (Excel, CSV, JSON) is fine; a one-time snapshot is fine

If the vessel-level dataset isn't available externally, I would also be very grateful for guidance on a workable alternative — an aggregated risk score per vessel, or pointers on applying your methodology against our existing AIS coverage.

Happy to share more about the project, our team, and our planned data-handling on request.
